# Bryophyte Sexual Systems: Monoicous vs. Dioicous

**Compiled:** 2026-03-10
**Purpose:** Summary of sexual systems (monoicous/dioicous) for bryophyte genome project species list
**Note:** Species with trailing numbers (e.g., "Tetraphis pellucida 1011") represent different accessions of the same taxon and share the same sexual system classification.

---

## Terminology

- **Dioicous (dioecious):** Antheridia and archegonia borne on separate male and female plants. ~57–60% of mosses, ~68% of liverworts.
- **Monoicous (monoecious):** Both sexes on the same gametophyte. Subtypes:
  - *Autoicous* — male and female branches separate but on same plant
  - *Paroicous* — antheridia immediately below archegonial head
  - *Synoicous* — antheridia and archegonia mixed in the same head
  - *Phyllodioicous* — dwarf male plants epiphytic on female

**Confidence key:** ✓✓ = high (confirmed by species-level source) | ✓ = medium (genus/family-level inference) | ? = uncertain (insufficient data)
